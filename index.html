Aims and scope

Hands-on with the Higgs Discovery. Understand the experimental effort and challenges on the theory side. Familiarize with techniques that are used to claim a discovery and what it means to measure the Higgs mass or couplings.

Audience: For Freshmen and Juniors. Knowledge of Freshmen Physics, introductory Quantum Mechanics but no Quantum Field Theory or Particle Physics knowledge is expected.

Topics and Schedule

Classes are scheduled for Mondays at 2pm in the CCPP lounge (Meyer building, 5th floor).

  1. February 3: CERN, LHC, ATLAS, CMS, Geneva. Big Science. Introduction to Particle Physics.

  2. February 10: Theories of nature with symmetry. Fundamental Particles and the Standard Model. Particle detectors and data acquisition.
    .
    No class on February 17.

  3. February 24: Particle masses. The Higgs boson and electroweak symmetry breaking. Hadron colliders. Higgs production modes and decay channels.

  4. March 3: Simulating the LHC. Statistical modeling to test the Higgs hypothesis. Understand LHC results. Discovery. Properties.

  5. (March 10)
